Prioritized actions for Telkomsel

Ranked by raw negative-comment volume among actionable topics (Telkomsel is the only brand with net-negative sentiment, so it's the one with a clear improvement case). "Other" and near-zero-complaint topics (Corporate & Brand, Events & CSR) are excluded from ranking — they aren't the problem.

1

Package & Pricing — clarify what's actually being sold

90 comments · 86% negative · 77 negative comments (highest of any topic)
  • Fix or clearly disclose the "1-day internet package = 12 hours only" gap — this single complaint was posted 23 times across 6 wordings, the single largest recurring theme in the dataset.
  • Disclose the 28-day billing cycle up front (several comments expect "30 hari" and feel shorted).
  • Investigate "pulsa tersedot" reports — multiple independent comments describe balance draining with no matching purchase.
2

Customer Service — stop escalations from going public and viral

95 comments · 79% negative · 75 negative comments
  • The "senior manager never called back" / Telkomsel Poin complaint was posted 23 times across 7 wordings (including English, aimed at international visibility) — a single unresolved case amplified into the dataset's most repeated complaint. Fast-track visible unresolved tickets before they get reposted.
  • Several comments say the DM assistant ("Vero" AI) never routes to a human — review the bot-to-agent handoff criteria.
  • Audit the Telkomsel Poin redemption flow specifically; it's named directly in multiple complaints as broken.
3

Network Experience — target power-outage drop-outs and event-day load

75 comments · 87% negative (highest negative rate of the four) · 65 negative comments
  • Multiple independent comments report signal dropping specifically when local power ("mati lampu") goes out — check backup power/battery runtime at affected BTS sites.
  • World Cup/MaxStream streaming complaints cluster with signal complaints during live matches — confirm backhaul capacity for high-profile streaming events.
  • Repeated city/district-level "when is 5G coming" requests suggest a public rollout roadmap would reduce repeat complaints even where no fix is imminent.
4

App & Account — unblock people who already paid

32 comments · 69% negative · 22 negative comments (lowest volume of the four, still worth fixing)
  • OTP delivery delays/failures block registration and number reactivation in several comments.
  • eSIM QR-code delivery delays leave paying customers unable to activate — prioritize fixes on the post-purchase activation path specifically.
Keep doing: Corporate & Brand and Events & CSR content (World Cup nobar events, UMKM/CSR campaigns, company-anniversary posts) together run 83% positive and only 3.5% negative — Corporate & Brand posts alone are 92% positive, while Events & CSR skews more neutral (fans naming World Cup teams) than negative. Either way, this content is what's holding Telkomsel's overall sentiment up despite the operational issues above, so don't deprioritize it while fixing the rest.

Methodology & caveats

  • Same pipeline across all three brands: Apify's instagram-scraper actor pulled posts from the last ~30 days, then comments per post (capped ~15/post on the free tier), via the same script used for the original XLSMART run.
  • Every unique comment text was individually read and labeled by hand for sentiment and topic — not a keyword classifier. For Telkomsel's much larger volume (634 raw comments), exact-duplicate text was labeled once and applied to all repeats, and the 176 comments made up only of emoji/symbols (no letters) were bulk-tagged Corporate & Brand / positive rather than read individually.
  • Each brand's own official reply comments were excluded; for Telkomsel and Indosat no official replies were detected in-window, for XLSMART 5 were removed.
  • Volumes differ a lot by brand (173 / 180 / 634) — normalized shares (%) are more comparable across brands than raw counts. Telkomsel's larger post count (55 vs. 31 and 16) is itself a difference in posting cadence, not just audience size.
  • Several near-identical complaints were posted repeatedly by the same or coordinated accounts (XLSMART's 10× signal-outage comment, Indosat's 11× spam-call template, Telkomsel's "senior manager" escalation posted 23 times across 7 wording variants, and its "12-hour package" complaint posted 23 times across 6 variants) — each is counted as written, so treat these as concentrated single incidents amplifying a topic's negative share, not as independent customer voices.
  • A handful of Telkomsel/Indosat comments were off-topic spam (land-dispute legal letters, debt-collection call-outs, bank-voting contest entries) and were bucketed as "Other" rather than forced into a telco topic.
