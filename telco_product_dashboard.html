Methodology & caveats

  • 14 Jul refresh: all three official websites were re-crawled today and checked against the prior snapshot — no pricing, quota, or line-up changes were found for any operator (XLSMART's Xtra Combo Flex tiers, Indosat's Freedom Internet rate card, and Telkomsel's gated pricing flow all matched the 10 Jul data). Instagram scraping could not be re-run this cycle — the Apify account hit its $5/month usage hard cap partway through the Telkomsel comment pull (cycle resets 8 Aug 2026) — so the product-post sentiment section and Telkomsel's Instagram-sourced catalog entries below still reflect the 10 Jul snapshot, not today.
  • Canonical official website source, corrected 14 Jul: going forward the designated official source per operator is telkomsel.com (unchanged), ioh.co.id/ID/home for Indosat, and xlsmart.co.id/id for XLSMART — replacing im3.id and xl.co.id respectively. That switch has not yet been executed against these two operators (blocked by the Apify usage cap above) — the Indosat and XLSMART figures below are still from im3.id/xl.co.id and need re-verification against the new canonical domains on the next successful crawl, including whether xlsmart.co.id (previously a content-free holding page, per the note below) now carries real product data.
  • Websites were crawled with a JavaScript-rendering scraper (Apify's website-content-crawler, up to 20 pages/site) since all three sites are client-side-rendered apps that return empty shells to a plain HTTP fetch.
  • Telkomsel's actual package/pricing page requires entering a phone number before showing any price — it could not be scraped generically. Telkomsel's product figures here come entirely from its official Instagram captions.
  • As of the last crawl, XLSMART's own domain (xlsmart.co.id) was a holding-company page with no product data — the XL line-up shown here was sourced from xl.co.id, which it links to. Smartfren and Axis were not crawled in this pass. This is being re-checked per the note above.
  • Indosat's rate-card table came from an emergency-top-up ("Pulsa SOS") page on im3.id, not a dedicated price list — numbers may include a small emergency-credit surcharge versus buying the package directly. Being re-sourced from ioh.co.id per the note above.
  • Hashtag search for #iconnet and #xtracomboflex returned mostly third-party reseller/agent posts (phone numbers, WhatsApp sales links), not organic customer or official content. Of 6 higher-quality posts selected for comment scraping, 5 were already private/deleted and the sixth yielded a single non-sentiment comment — so Indosat and XLSMART product sentiment is genuinely not measurable from public data this window, not just "not posted."
  • This pass also attempted official TikTok, official X/Twitter, and the Meta Ad Library for each operator (see "Data source coverage" above). TikTok scraping works technically but returned only entertainment/CSR/brand content across all three accounts — no pricing, quota, or launch news to add. X/Twitter and Meta Ad Library were not retrievable with the current tooling/account tier, not surveyed and found empty — treat those two as unverified, not "confirmed no activity."
  • Brand colors are sampled from each operator's own logo file; Telkomsel and Indosat both brand in red, so their chart colors are differentiated by shade — logos are the primary identity signal throughout, color is secondary.
  • Operator logos are pulled directly from each brand's own official site (telkomsel.com, xl.co.id, im3.id) and displayed at fixed height with native aspect ratio preserved.
  • This is a snapshot meant to be refreshed daily — re-run before using this for a live pricing decision.
